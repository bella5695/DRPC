Using DRPC Resources

One of the goals of the DRPC is to create resources that intsructors can use or adapt to, in order to talk about digital privacy in their classrooms. This section of the DRPC website provides information on how to cite DRPC resources that you use, and how to list them on your CV or resume. It also lists any adaptations and other course materials from instructors that used DRPC resources in their writing classrooms. Do you use DRPC resources in your classroom? Please reach out to us and tell us how via our Contact page!

How To Cite The DRPC When You Use Resources Archived With Us:

This is how to cite resources from the DRPC that you use from us:

This is the format for citing with us: 


    Full credit for the concept and 
    design of [assignment name] goes to
    [author’s name], whose work is archived 
    in the Digital Rhetorical Privacy 
    Collective resources (linked here).

How To Document Your Contributions To DRPC On Your CV Or Resume:

This is the format for documenting your contributions to DRPC on your CV or resume: Last Name, First Name. (Year, Month Date) Title of Contribution. The Digital Rhetorical Privacy Collective. Hyperlink.

Example of the format with the DRPC: 


    Woods, Charles, Gray, Brenna C.,
    & Hope, Lacy. (2022, June 30). 
    Privacy + surveillance & course hero.
    The Digital Rhetorical Privacy 
    Collective. drpcollective.com.

Note: If listing your contribution as a publication doesn’t feel like a right fit, consider listing your work in one of these sections on your CV or resume like this: 


   Affiliations 
   Service (to the field, to the public) 
   Public scholarship or Public Pedagogy
