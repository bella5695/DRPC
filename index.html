DRPC - Digital Rhetorical Privacy Collective

The Digital Rhetorical Privacy Collective is a team that approaches studying, privacy,
and surveillance within rhetoric, composition, and technical communication.
The goals and mission of the organization is to collect and redistribute academic
teaching resources and discuss about educational topics for our audience.
We won the CCCC Emerging Researcher Award in 2023.
In 2024, we are planning to continue expanding our resources to the public.

“Teaching About Privacy
in an Age of Generative AI“


Date: Monday, January 22, 2024
Time: 4:00pm-5:00pm EST
Podcast: Zoom Meeting

Register Here

Utah State University presents: The Digital Rhetorical Privacy Collective Privacy Week 2024, “Teaching about privacy in the age of generative AI”. This is a virtual panel hosted in Utah State organized by Dr. Chen Chen and sponsored by USU College of Humanities and Social Sciences, USU Libraries, USU Department of English, Department of Journalism and Communication. The panel will feature: Dr. Calvin Pollak (University of Washington): “Navigating Publicity and Privacy: Genre-Based Technical Communication Pedagogy With, For, and Against ChatGPT”; Reed Hepler (Digital Initiatives Librarian and Archivist at College of Southern Idaho): “Deliberately Safeguarding Privacy and Confidentiality in the Era of Generative AI”; and, Dr. Atilla Hallsby (University of Minnesota): “We Live in Public: Teaching Publicity, Privacy, and Secrecy with OpenSecrets.org and Market Research Data.”

Thursday, January 25, 2024: “Black Feminism, Reproductive Justice, and Digital Surveillance

4:30pm-5:30pm EST – Tawes Building Room 2115 at UMD and Zoom

University of Maryland–College Park: The @UMDEnglish Language, Writing, & Rhetoric Event Series in collaboration with the Digical Rhetorical Privacy Collective present: “Reproduction & Medical Technology in the Age of ‘Crisis’” a talk by Dr. Mali Collins. This talk investigates the rhetoric of “crisis” as it pertains to the “Black Maternal Health Crisis”, and the ways ‘crisis” navigates as a bull horn to necessitate the over medicalization and mis-/and overuse of reproductive technologies on and for black birthing people.
